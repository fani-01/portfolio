    System Comparison

    Federated vs Centralized Systems

    Toggle to understand exactly why federated learning is the only viable approach for multi-bank fraud detection.

    ❌ Centralized Architecture — Why It Fails

    All raw bank transaction data sent to one central server. Legally impossible, catastrophically risky, commercially unacceptable.

    • 🔴Raw customer PAN, accounts, transaction history, location transmitted over networks — violates banking secrecy laws
    • 🔴Single point of failure — one breach exposes ALL banks' data simultaneously with zero recovery possible
    • 🔴Violates GDPR, RBI Data Localization 2018, India's DPDP Act 2023, PCI-DSS v4.0
    • 🔴Banks cannot legally share proprietary customer data with competitors or third-party processors
    • 🔴Gradient inversion attacks can reconstruct individual records from aggregated data
    • 🔴Petabyte-scale transmission costs — economically unsustainable for real-time analysis
    What Gets Exposed
    💾 DATA IN TRANSIT
    Account numbers · PAN cards · Full names · Transaction history · Beneficiaries · Merchant data · GPS location · Device fingerprints
    🎯 ATTACK SURFACE
    1 central server = 1 target. 1 breach = 100% exposure across all banks. Estimated damage: ₹500Cr+
    ⚖️ REGULATORY PENALTIES
    GDPR: €20M or 4% annual turnover · RBI: License suspension · DPDP 2023: ₹250Cr per breach · PCI-DSS: Processing ban

    ✅ Federated Learning — The Solution

    Each bank trains locally. Only encrypted, noise-added gradient vectors travel to the aggregator. Full accuracy. Zero privacy compromise. Fully legal.

    • 🟢Zero raw data leaves any bank — only mathematical gradient vectors with Gaussian noise (differential privacy)
    • 🟢Distributed resilience — 1 bank fails → 9 others continue operating and training independently
    • 🟢Fully compliant: GDPR, RBI Localization, DPDP Act 2023, PCI-DSS v4.0, banking secrecy laws
    • 🟢Banks maintain complete data sovereignty while sharing collective fraud intelligence
    • 🟢FedAvg secure aggregation — mathematically impossible to reverse-engineer individual bank gradients
    • 🟢Differential privacy ε < 1.0 per round — formal mathematical proof of privacy guarantees

    Privacy Proofs

    Every claim is backed by formal mathematical guarantees — not policy promises.

    🔒
    Differential Privacy

    Gaussian mechanism injects calibrated noise into gradient vectors. Guarantees (ε,δ)-DP with ε < 1.0 per round. Even with all shared gradients, no adversary can determine if any individual's data was in the training set.

    Secure Aggregation (FedAvg)

    Cryptographic masking — each bank's gradient masked with one-time pads shared with other banks. Aggregator sees only the combined sum. Individual bank gradients are computationally unrecoverable.
